📖 Working Method

Open Space Technology

Instead of sitting through a pre-built agenda, you build it. The topics that matter most to this team, right now, get the airtime.

⚙️

How It Works

🛒 The Marketplace

Each Open Space block begins with the full group in a circle. Peter frames the central question for that block. Then anyone can propose a topic — something they want to discuss, solve, or explore. You don't need to be an expert. You just need to care enough to raise it and host a conversation about it.

Each proposed topic gets a time slot and a space (a corner, a table, a flip chart). This creates a real-time agenda built by the team, not by leadership.

🧭

The Four Principles

1
Whoever comes is the right people
If three people show up to your session, those are the three who care most. That's the right conversation.
2
Whatever happens is the only thing that could have
Don't get attached to a specific outcome. Follow the energy where it leads.
3
Whenever it starts is the right time
Creativity and insight don't run on a clock. Don't force it if it's not ready.
4
When it's over, it's over
If a conversation wraps in 20 minutes, it's done. Don't stretch it. Note it and bring it back if needed.
👣

The Law of Two Feet

👣 The Most Important Rule

If you find yourself in a session where you are neither learning nor contributing, use your two feet. Go to another session. Take a walk. Sit and think. This is not rude — it's expected and respected. It means every conversation has people who genuinely want to be there.

🎯

Retreat Objectives

1

Turn a Distributed Group Into a Real Team

Not "bonding" — operating trust. Psychological safety to disagree, understanding how others think and decide, and normalizing productive tension. Outcome: People leave knowing how to work together, not just what they're working on.

2

Create Ruthless Clarity on Near-Term Priorities

The next 6 months. What matters most, what explicitly does not, and what tradeoffs we're making on purpose. Outcome: Fewer Slack pings, fewer re-litigation loops, faster execution.

3

Make Ownership, Decision-Making & Interfaces Explicit

Who owns what. Who supports. Who decides. When to escalate vs. decide locally. Outcome: Less frustration, less duplication, more autonomy.

4

Establish Team Norms & Shared Vocabulary

Feedback norms, accountability norms, how conflict shows up, what "good" looks like here — not generically. Outcome: Consistency without micromanagement.

🔥

Pain Points Worth Raising

These themes have surfaced in planning conversations. You don't have to limit yourself to these — anyone can raise anything. But if you're wondering "what kind of thing should I propose?" — this is the territory:

⚡

Summit Recognition Ceremony

A new Tiger Tracks tradition — honoring every person on this team for who they are and what they bring.

🪙 25 Commemorative Coins · One for Every Attendee
✨

About This Tradition

This event celebrates our unique qualities and contributions. By witnessing this tradition, others gain a deeper understanding of each person — who they are, what they stand for, what they bring to this team that no one else does.

As a token of our appreciation, each attendee receives a special commemorative coin — a physical reminder of this week and their place on this team.

📋

How the Ceremony Runs

1

Circle Up — Everyone in the Room

After the Closing Session (Personal Commitments), the full team gathers in a circle. No screens, phones away. This is for each other.

2

One Person at a Time

Each of the 25 team members is honored individually. The group shares what they appreciate most about that person — their unique quality, their specific contribution, the thing that only they bring to Tiger Tracks.

3

The Person Being Honored Can Respond

One or two sentences — whatever feels right. No pressure to give a speech. Even silence is fine. The point is receiving, not performing.

4

🪙 Coin Presentation

Cliff, Henry, or Elizabeth presents the commemorative coin to the honoree. A handshake, a hug — whatever feels right for that person. The coin represents their place on this team and their contribution to this week.

5

Repeat for All 25

Every single person. No one is skipped. The ceremony typically runs 30–45 minutes for a group of 25 when each recognition is kept to 2–3 minutes.

💡

Tips for a Great Recognition

🎯
Be specific, not generic. "You're hardworking" is forgettable. "The way you stayed calm when the client called on a Friday at 4pm and turned it around by Monday" is something they'll carry.
👀
Speak to what only they do. What quality does this person have that makes Tiger Tracks better in a way no one else could replicate?
🤝
Anyone can speak. It doesn't have to be leadership. The most powerful recognitions often come from peers who see each other's work up close.
🧘
Let it land. After each recognition, pause for a moment before moving to the next person. The silence is part of it.
🪙
