📖 The Format

Open Space Technology

Instead of sitting through a pre-packaged agenda, the team builds it. The topics that matter most to this specific group, right now, get the airtime. Leadership doesn't control the agenda — the room does.

⚙️

How It Works

Each Open Space block begins with the full group in a circle. Peter frames the central question for that block. Then anyone can propose a topic — something they want to discuss, solve, or explore. You don't need to be an expert. You just need to care enough to raise it and own a conversation.

Each proposed topic gets a time slot and a space. This creates a real-time agenda built by the room — not by leadership, not by a consulting firm, not in advance.
🧭

The Four Principles

Whoever comes is the right people
If three people show up to your session, those are the three who care most. That's the right conversation — full stop.
Whatever happens is the only thing that could
Don't get attached to a specific outcome. Follow the energy where it actually wants to go.
Whenever it starts is the right time
Creativity doesn't run on a clock. If the good stuff starts at 10:14, it starts at 10:14.
When it's over, it's over
Done in 20 minutes? It's done. Close it, capture it, move on. Don't stretch a finished conversation.
👣

The Law of Two Feet

👣 The Rule That Makes It Work

If you're in a session where you are neither learning nor contributing, use your two feet. Go to another session. Sit and think. Take a walk. This is not rude. It's respected. It means every conversation has people who genuinely want to be there — and that makes every conversation better.

🎯

What We're Here to Accomplish

1
Turn a distributed group into a real operating team
Not "bonding" — actual trust. Where you know how your teammates think and make decisions without having to check every move.
2
Get ruthlessly clear on what matters in the next 6 months
Not "everything is important." What specifically are we doing, not doing, and why? No more re-litigating priorities every Monday.
3
Make ownership, decision-making, and interfaces explicit
Who owns what. Who decides. When to escalate vs. just move. Fewer Slack pings. More autonomy. Less duplication.
4
Establish team norms and shared vocabulary
How feedback works here. How accountability works here. What "good" looks like — for this specific team. Not generically. Actually.
🔥

Summit Recognition Ceremony

A new Tiger Tracks tradition — honoring every single person for who they are and what they bring.

🪙 25 Commemorative Coins · One for Every Attendee
✨

About This Tradition

This ceremony celebrates what makes each person on this team uniquely valuable. By witnessing each recognition, the rest of the team gains a deeper understanding of who each person is — not just what they do, but what they bring that no one else could.

Each attendee receives a commemorative coin — a physical token of this week and their place on this team. This is the first year. It becomes our annual tradition.
📋

How the Ceremony Works

1

Circle Up — Full Team, Phones Away

After the closing session, everyone gathers in a circle. No screens. This is for each other.

2

One Person at a Time

Each of the 25 team members is honored individually. The group shares what they appreciate most — their unique quality, their specific contribution, the thing only they bring to Tiger Tracks.

3

The Honoree Can Respond

One or two sentences. Or silence. The point is receiving, not performing. Let it land.

4

🪙 Coin Presentation

Cliff, Henry, or Elizabeth presents the commemorative coin. A handshake, a hug — whatever feels right. The coin represents their place on this team.

5

Repeat for All 25

Every. Single. Person. Nobody skipped. ~2–3 minutes per person. Total: ~45 minutes for 25 people.

💡

Tips for a Powerful Recognition

🎯
Be specific, not generic. "You're great" is forgettable. "The way you handled the client escalation on a Friday at 4pm and had it solved by Monday" — that lands.
👀
Name what only they do. What quality makes Tiger Tracks better in a way nobody else could replicate?
🤝
Anyone can speak. Not just leadership. The most powerful recognitions usually come from peers who see the work up close.
🧘
Let it land. Pause between each person. The silence is part of it. Don't rush to the next one.
🪙
